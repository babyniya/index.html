CULTURAL EVOLUTION OF ASIAN CIVILIZATION

Paleolithic Age

Early people used their intelligence to develop technology.
Technology refers to a group's skill in using tools. Tools made man's
survival easier. The invention of tools made of stones, use of fire, and the growth of language
are important achievements in human history. These achievements
during the prehistoric period is known as the Stone Age or the Paleolithic Age which was about 2 million years ago. The term
"paleolithic" was derived from the Greek word "palaios," which means old, and "litho," which
means stone. During this stage, the early people were nomads. Nomads are those people who wanders from place to place.
They did not have a permanent home. They walked erect and had the physical characteristicsof a human being.
They hunted for food. The following are their important characteristics which made the
early humans different from animals.

Nearly all of our knowledge of Paleolithic people and way of life comes from
archaelogy and ethnographic comparisons to modern hunter gatherer cultures such
as Kung San who live similarly to their Paleolithic precedecessors.
The economy of a typical Paleolithic society was a hunter-gatherer economy.
Humans hunted wild animals for meat and gathered food, firewood, and materials for their tools, clothes, or shelters.
