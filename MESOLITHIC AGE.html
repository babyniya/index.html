Mesolithic Age

The transition from Old Stone Age to the New Stone Age is known as
the Mesolithic period, there were changes in climatic conditions. Animals in particular,
became smaller in size. This small size enabled the animals
to move faster. Because of this, man had to develop
lighter tools to aid them in hunting. Hunting and gathering plants
continued to be the main source of food
until they began to store food in storages for later consumption. Archaeological
findings and wall paintings proved that domestication of animals,
specifically dogs, was the main development of this period.
