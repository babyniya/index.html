The Neolithic Revolution

The second phase of the Stone Age is known as the New Stone Age or
Neolithic Age. This began 10,000 years ago. At this time, man made
more polished stone tools.
They also made potteries and started
to cultivate crops

In the event of the growth of population, it was believed that
agricultural revolution also occured. Scientists concluded that
this might have caused by the change in climate. Rising temperatures
worldwide provided longer growing seasons. With the continuous growth
of population, man was forced to find ways to supply their needs.
Thus, it was believed that they continuosly attempted to farm.
Farming provided a steady source of food.
During the Neolithic Age:
-Man learned to polish his tools to a fine edge and sharp
points.
-Man discovered the importance of settling down in
villages, domesticating animals, and planting their own food. Due to the uncertainty
of food supply in the Paleolithic Age, agriculture became the greatest
discovery of man. Agricultural productivity led to a production boom.
-Man settled in permanent homes. As man learned agriculture, he also learned
to live in permnent places such as villages and started to build shelters. This probably
became the reason for the intentiveness and creativity of man. They started to build furniture,
make potteries, and weave clothing. More people lived together and formed
communities. This led to the creation of rules and laws to act as social control.
This also created the first organized governments.
-Man began to develop shared ways of doing things as they began
to live near one another. This is caled culture. Culture refers to the way of life of a group of people
in a certain place.
It is the sum of total of the learned behaviour of a group that are generally considered to be
the tradition of those people
and are transmitted from generation to generation.
