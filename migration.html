The Early Human Migration

The early transition from hunting and gathering to farming
and herding took place in many places. This proved that early
humans had migrated in many parts of the world. As the
migration map show, humans struggled to move into West Asia,
North Asia, and Central Asia thousands of years ago. They travelled
Through East Asia, South Asia, and Southeast Asia.

Jarmo in West Asia (presently Iraq) and Catal Huyuk (presently in
Turkey) were good examples of the early village settlements. Jericho was the oldest city
ever discovered. Since 1989, a team led by Ufuk Esin
of Istanbul University has been excavating Asikli.
Asikli is a village believed to be about 1000 years
older than Catal Huyuk.

It was British archaeologist James Mellaart who discovered
Catal Huyuk in 1958. According to Mellaart, the site
is 4500 years older than the pyramids of Egypt. On
the other hand, the excavation of Jericho was first carried out by
British archaeologist Kathleen Kenyon in the 1950s.

Catal Huyuk is a Neolithic town located in South Central Turkey.
As described, the city layout does not show traces of streets,
but it shows very evident structures of brick houses which
which are positioned alongside each other. The passage into
the houses is through an opening on the roof.
Located between two volcanoes, the village was known for
its abundant obsidian products and dark volcanic rock
used to make mirrors, jewelries, and knives.

The excavation of Catal Huyuk also revealed illustrated
wall paintings of animals and hunting scenes. The painting also
showed erupting volcano and hunters slaying wild
cattles and other wild animals. Artifacts like stone beads,
grinding equipment, native copper, lead beads
and ground obsidian mirrors were founf at the site. An
artifact is an object produced or shaped by human skill,
such as a tool, weapon, or ornament of archaeological
or historical interest.
